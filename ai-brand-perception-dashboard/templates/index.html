Methodology & Scoring Explanation
▼

VADER Sentiment

Compound scores range from −1 (most negative) to +1 (most positive). Articles ≥ 0.05 = Positive, ≤ −0.05 = Negative, else Neutral.

Brand Health Score

Computed as (Positive − Negative) / Total × 100, normalised to a 0–100 scale. Higher scores indicate stronger public perception.

Risk Classification

Negative ratio > 40% = High Risk. 20–40% = Moderate Risk. Below 20% = Low Risk. Based on proportion of negative articles.

Volatility (σ)

Standard deviation of VADER compound scores across all articles. High σ indicates unstable or polarised brand coverage.

Confidence Level

Based on article count. ≥ 15 articles = High, 8–14 = Medium, < 8 = Low. More articles produce more reliable scores.

Trend Forecast

3-point linear regression forecast plotted as a dashed extension on the sentiment trend chart. Uses least-squares extrapolation.
